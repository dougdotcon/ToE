Neutrino Oscillations: Unknots with Tiny Deformations

Douglas H. M. Fulber • UFRJ • January 2026
Abstract Neutrinos oscillate between flavors, proving they have mass. In TARDIS, neutrinos are "unknots" (trivial topology, genus 0). The three mass eigenstates differ by tiny deformations (twist/writhe), and flavor mixing arises from how unknots couple to charged leptons. The solar mixing angle θ₁₂ ≈ 33° hints at tribimaximal symmetry.

1. Unknots and Mixing

Mixing angles: θ₁₂ = 33.4°, θ₂₃ = 49.2°, θ₁₃ = 8.6°. The near-maximal θ₂₃ and small θ₁₃ suggest underlying geometric symmetry in unknot deformations.
