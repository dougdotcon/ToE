Abstract

We propose a phenomenological limit to quantum linearity based on the holographic information capacity of spacetime. By treating the Universal Compression Parameter ($\Omega \approx 117.038$) as a fundamental bound on information density, we derive a Universal Critical Mass ($M_c$) for spontaneous wavefunction collapse. The model predicts $M_c \approx M_P \cdot \Omega^{-4} \approx 1.25 \times 10^{-16}$ kg. Objects exceeding this mass scale cannot maintain coherent superposition states for timescales longer than the Planck time scaled by $\Omega$. Unlike subjective collapse models, this framework provides a parameter-free geometric threshold derived directly from the proposed entropic structure of the universe. We delineate specific experimental falsification criteria using next-generation matter-wave interferometry and optomechanics.

1. Introduction: The Limits of Linearity

Standard Quantum Mechanics (SQM) assumes unitary evolution ($\hat{U} = e^{-i\hat{H}t/\hbar}$) applies to systems of arbitrary mass and complexity. However, the measurement problem and the absence of macroscopic superpositions ("Schrödinger's Cat" states) suggest a breakdown of unitarity at a fundamental scale. Models like GRW (Ghirardi-Rimini-Weber) and Penrose's Gravitational Orchestrated Reduction (Orch-OR) introduce ad-hoc parameters or gravitational arguments to enforce classicality.

We propose that the breakdown of unitarity is not ad-hoc but a direct consequence of the finite information capacity of the holographic horizon. Just as the Bekenstein bound limits the entropy of a black hole, we postulate a "Coherence Bound" for any localized region of spacetime, governed by the dimensionless structural constant $\Omega$.

2. The Critical Mass Hypothesis

2.1 The Ansatz

We postulate that the transition from Quantum (Unitary) to Classical (Entropic) dynamics occurs when the gravitational self-energy of a superposition state exceeds a holographic bit-threshold defined by $\Omega$. Phenomenologically, this identifies a critical mass scale $M_c$ derived from the Planck mass ($M_P$):

$$ M_c \equiv M_P \cdot \Omega^{-\delta} $$

where $\delta$ is a topological scaling dimension. Based on the 4-dimensional nature of bulk spacetime (or equivalently, the specific volume-to-boundary mapping), we set $\delta = 4$ as a working hypothesis necessary to recover the mesoscopic transition scale.

2.2 Calculation

Using $M_P \approx 2.1764 \times 10^{-8}$ kg and the phenomenological value $\Omega = 117.038$:

$$ M_c \approx (2.1764 \times 10^{-8} \text{ kg}) \times (117.038)^{-4} $$ $$ \Omega^4 \approx 1.876 \times 10^8 $$ $$ M_c \approx \frac{2.1764 \times 10^{-8}}{1.876 \times 10^8} \approx 1.16 \times 10^{-16} \text{ kg} $$

This value ($\sim 10^{-16}$ kg or $0.1$ picograms) sits squarely in the "mesoscopic gap" currently being probed by advanced optomechanics and macromolecule interferometry.

3. Mechanism of Collapse

In this framework, a superposition of a massive object at two distinct locations $|L\rangle + |R\rangle$ creates an ambiguity in the spacetime geometry (a "bifurcation" of the holographic screen). The energy cost to maintain this bifurcation is entropic.

When $M < M_c$, the information cost is negligible compared to the horizon's thermal fluctuations, allowing unitarity to hold effectively. When $M \ge M_c$, the geometric ambiguity exceeds the system's ability to maintain coherence, forcing a rapid, non-unitary decay into either $|L\rangle$ or $|R\rangle$. Collapse is an entropy maximization process.

6. Conclusion

We have defined a testable boundary for the validity of quantum mechanics, the Universal Critical Mass $M_c \approx 1.16 \times 10^{-16}$ kg. This value is not arbitrary but is derived from the same structural parameter $\Omega = 117.038$ that organizes the electron mass and fine-structure constant in earlier works.

This proposal shifts the "Theory of Everything" from a mathematical curiosity to an experimental target. If superpositions of $10^{-15}$ kg objects are observed, our fundamental assumption of an entropic holographic bound is false. Conversely, the discovery of a collapse threshold at this specific scale would provide strong evidence for the informational/geometric nature of reality proposed by the TARDIS framework.
